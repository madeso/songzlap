# Arrangement

The arrangement is a 32-bar timeline. Each track appears as a row, and clips (coloured blocks) represent sections of music.

---

## Tracks

### Adding a Track

Click **+ Track** in the top-left corner of the arrangement area. A new track appears with a default synthesizer sound.

Each track shows:
- Its **name** (truncated if long — hover to see the full name)
- A coloured bar on the left edge
- A dropdown to choose which **instrument** it uses
- A **✎ icon** to open the Instrument Editor for that instrument
- A **mute button** (🔊 / 🔇) to silence the track
- A **✕ button** to delete the track

### Selecting a Track

Click a track name to select it (highlighted with a darker background). Click again to deselect. Only one track can be selected at a time. The selected track is the one that plays in **Track** playback mode.

### Muting a Track

Click the mute button (🔊) to silence a track. The icon changes to 🔇. Muted tracks are skipped during playback and are not included in WAV exports. Click again to unmute.

### Removing a Track

Click the **✕** button on the track. This deletes the track and all its clips.

---

## Clips

A clip is a block of notes placed on the timeline. Each clip is 4 bars long and snaps to bar boundaries.

### Placing a Clip

**Left-click on an empty spot** in the arrangement grid to place a new clip. You can't place overlapping clips on the same track.

Each clip shows a miniature preview of the notes inside it — small dots that give you a quick sense of pitch density.

### Opening a Clip in the Piano Roll

**Left-click on an existing clip** to open it in the Piano Roll. The Piano Roll appears at the bottom of the screen.

### Removing a Clip

**Right-click on a clip** to delete it from the timeline.

> **Note:** Removing a clip is permanent. If it was the only placement of that clip, its notes are lost.
