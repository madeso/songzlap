# Piano Roll

The Piano Roll opens at the bottom of the screen when you click a clip. It's where you draw and edit the notes inside that clip.

The grid shows pitch vertically (higher notes at the top) and time horizontally (left to right). The clip spans 4 bars.

---

## Opening and Closing

- **Open:** Left-click any clip in the arrangement.
- **Close:** Click **×** in the Piano Roll header.

---

## Navigating

The piano keyboard on the left side labels every C and F. The roll covers **C2 to B5** — four octaves.

- **Scroll vertically** to move through the octave range.
- **Scroll horizontally** to move through the 4 bars.

The roll auto-scrolls to centre around C4 when it opens.

---

## Drawing Notes

**Click and drag** to the right on an empty area of the grid. A violet preview shows the note as you drag. Release to place it.

Notes snap to **16th-note** increments.

If you click without dragging, the note is placed using the **last-used length**.

---

## Note Length

The Piano Roll header shows the current note length — for example `1/4`, `1/8`, `1/16`. This updates automatically as you draw or resize notes, and becomes the default for the next single-click.

---

## Resizing Notes

Hover near the **right edge** of a note — the cursor changes to a resize arrow (↔). Click and drag left or right to shorten or lengthen the note. Snaps to 16th-note increments.

---

## Removing Notes

Click on the **body** of a note (not the right edge) to delete it.

> **Tip:** There is no undo — deleted notes are gone immediately.

---

## Hint

The Piano Roll header always shows: *"Drag to draw · Drag right edge to resize · Click note to remove"*
